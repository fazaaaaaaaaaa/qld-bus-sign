🚌 Flash your QLD Bus Sign

Xteink X4 · ESP32-C3 · one-click installer

This installs the live Brisbane bus-departure firmware onto your Xteink X4 directly from this page — no app, no toolchain.

Before you start

  1. Use Chrome or Edge on a desktop (Safari/Firefox can't talk to USB devices).
  2. Plug the X4 into this computer with a USB-C data cable (not a charge-only cable).
  3. No microSD card is needed — leave it out. The firmware lives on the internal flash.
Your browser can't flash USB devices. Open this page in desktop Chrome or Edge. Flashing is only allowed over HTTPS in Chrome/Edge.

What happens

  1. Click Connect & Install, then pick the X4's serial port (look for “USB JTAG/serial” or ESP32-C3). If nothing appears, re-seat the cable.
  2. Choose Install and confirm Erase device when asked (clean install over the stock firmware). Takes about a minute.
  3. When it finishes, unplug and re-plug, or just leave it powered.
After flashing — set up WiFi from your phone:
The sign creates a WiFi network called QLD-Bus-Sign-Setup. Join it on your phone; a setup page pops up automatically. Pick your WiFi and enter the password. The board then shows your departures — before 2 pm your home stop (Bonney Ave, route 320), from 2 pm the city stop (Adelaide St, 320 & 322). If the screen is upside-down, re-open that setup page and change rotation 1→3.
Heads-up: this replaces the X4's stock reader firmware. Future updates happen automatically over WiFi (OTA) — you won't need this page again.
Prefer the command line? Download firmware-merged.bin and run
esptool.py --chip esp32c3 write_flash 0x0 firmware-merged.bin
